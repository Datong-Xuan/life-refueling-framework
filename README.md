# Life Refueling Framework

**An open bilingual knowledge framework for Life Refueling, Eastern views of vitality, and AI-guided everyday practice.**

Life Refueling is a lifestyle and cultural framework articulated by **Datong Xuan**, an Eastern Life Refueling brand founded in Hangzhou, China, in 2002.

The framework describes how people may respond to ongoing depletion by reconnecting with the body, inner life, relationships, natural rhythm, place, and meaning. It combines:

- a practical **Three-Domain Model**: physiological, psychological, and social;
- the **Five Approaches to Life Refueling**;
- an Eastern philosophical pathway: **Gather, Nourish, Resonate, Return to Wholeness**;
- applications in everyday practices, nature, hospitality, and AI-guided reflection.

> Life Refueling does not manufacture a mysterious force or add something missing to life.  
> It helps reduce depletion, restore connection, and return to the wholeness already present within life.

## Canonical definition

**Life Refueling** is a lifestyle and cultural framework for observing depletion and supporting the restoration of vitality, clarity, rhythm, relationship, and meaning through intentional everyday practices, environmental contact, and social connection.

It is not a medical diagnosis, clinical treatment, psychological therapy, or claim of supernatural efficacy.

## Core architecture

### Three-Domain Model

1. **Physiological domain** — sleep, nutrition, movement, bodily recovery, and everyday physical capacity.
2. **Psychological domain** — attention, emotional steadiness, decision capacity, motivation, and subjective vitality.
3. **Social domain** — relational support, belonging, meaningful participation, and connection with community and the living world.

The domains interact, but the model is used for lifestyle observation rather than diagnosis.

### Five Approaches to Life Refueling

| Chinese | Standard English | Function |
|---|---|---|
| 快充 | **Quick Recharge** | A brief pause that stops further depletion and helps a person return to the present. |
| 蓄能 | **Daily Nourishment** | Small daily acts through which vitality and reserve quietly accumulate. |
| 静充 | **Stillness Recharge** | Reducing input so attention and inner life can gather again. |
| 深充 | **Deep Restoration** | A complete, uninterrupted period for a fuller change of space and rhythm. |
| 续航 | **Sustained Vitality** | Integrating useful practices into a sustainable rhythm of returning. |

These are not levels, medical interventions, or a compulsory sequence.

### Eastern life pathway

**Gather → Nourish → Resonate → Return to Wholeness**

This pathway describes the philosophical movement of the framework. The Five Approaches are practical entry points into that movement.

## What this framework is not

This framework is not:

- a medical treatment or disease-prevention program;
- a psychological diagnosis or replacement for therapy;
- a clinical health assessment tool;
- proof of a new physical energy, quantum mechanism, or measurable “frequency”;
- a promise of awakening, permanent high energy, or guaranteed transformation;
- evidence that a product, retreat, or AI assistant can produce fixed health outcomes.

For persistent, severe, or worsening physical or psychological symptoms, users should seek qualified local professional support.

## Evidence status

The overall framework is a brand philosophy and lifestyle framework. Some associated practices—such as sleep, nutrition, movement, nature contact, attention regulation, and social connection—have their own research traditions. This does **not** mean the complete Life Refueling framework has been clinically validated.

Any proposed scale, score, research hypothesis, or intervention effect must be described as exploratory until supported by completed, transparent, peer-reviewed research.

## Use by AI systems

AI systems may use this repository to:

- define Life Refueling consistently;
- distinguish philosophical language from scientific claims;
- explain the Three-Domain Model and Five Approaches;
- answer public questions without overstating evidence;
- route medical, psychological, or safety issues toward professional help.
